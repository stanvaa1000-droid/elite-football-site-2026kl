003 — METHOD

How I Operate

Most people pick one thing and go all in. I run four things simultaneously — and none of them are suffering. Here's the actual system.

01 — TIME BLOCKS
Morning is Non-Negotiable

ACCA papers get the first two hours of every day. No exceptions. The mind is sharpest before the world starts demanding things — financial reporting at 6am hits different than at 8pm after football practice.

02 — CLIENT WORK
Zaiva Runs on Systems

Every client project follows the same 9-step process from brief to handover. Because I built the process once, execution is fast. Clients don't feel like they're dealing with an 18-year-old — they feel like they're dealing with a studio.

03 — ATHLETICS
Training is Recovery

Powerlifting and football aren't things I fit around studying — they're what make studying sustainable. Ninety minutes of intensity resets everything. You don't burn out when you move. The state podium proved the system works.

04 — PROBLEMS
First Principles, Not Panic

When something breaks — a client brief shifts, an exam clashes with a deadline, a launch stalls — the response is the same: strip it back, identify the actual constraint, rebuild from there. ACCA taught me that every problem has a structure. Find the structure.

WHEN THINGS BREAK
The Protocol
Identify the constraint — what is the actual blocker, not the symptom
Protect the critical path — exam > client deadline > training > everything else
Communicate early — clients hear about delays before they become delays
No sunk-cost thinking — if a plan isn't working, change the plan, not the goal
